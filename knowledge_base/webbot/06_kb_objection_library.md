> **Owner:** Sales · **Update frequency:** As objections emerge from real conversations (continuously) · **Last verified:** Q1 2026
>
> This KB captures every real objection surfaced in past ASBL sales conversations, public forums, and competitor comparisons. Each has:
> (1) **Mirror** — one-clause acknowledgement
> (2) **Disrupt** — one fresh fact that punches through

## CATEGORY 1 — PRICING & FINANCIAL OBJECTIONS

### "Price is too high"
**Mirror:** *"Fair — ₹1.94 Cr deserves scrutiny."*
**Disrupt:** *"Box price is the wrong anchor. After ₹85K/month rental till Dec 2026, your net effective entry is closer to ₹1.73 Cr."*
**Math:** *"That's roughly ₹7,100/month off your future EMI. The home pays for itself while you wait."*
**Close:** *"Comparing to a specific project, or is it the absolute ticket?"*

### "I can get a bigger flat in Kokapet for this price"
**Mirror:** *"You can — and that's a genuine trade-off."*
**Disrupt:** *"Kokapet at ₹10,800/sqft is real, but Neopolis new launches are at ₹11,555+/sqft now. More importantly, your Kokapet tenant is junior staff at ₹40-50K; your FD tenant is a director-level GCC hire at ₹85K. The sqft is cheaper; the yield universe is completely different."*
**Close:** *"Is the pull to Kokapet about size, price, or something else like family/community?"*

### "What's the catch on the rental offer?"
**Mirror:** *"Good — sophisticated buyers always ask this."*
**Disrupt:** *"Contractual in the agreement, ₹50/sqft/month till 31 Dec 2026. Not a forecast. Not adjustable. The 'catch' is that the base price isn't discounted — you're paying market and getting cash flow on top."*
**Math:** *"12 months × ₹85K = ₹10.2L. Net effective entry drops from ₹1.94 Cr to ~₹1.73 Cr. No hidden charge, no claw-back clause."*
**Close:** *"Want me to walk you through how the clause sits in the agreement?"*

**Close:** *"Sales can connect you with a CA to optimize — want me to route that?"*

## META-RULE: COMPOSE, DON'T RECITE

These responses are **raw ammunition**, not word-for-word scripts. The bot composes naturally, varies vocabulary, adapts to the user's tone, and sometimes skips mirror or math if the conversation doesn't need all four steps. The framework is a scaffold, not a cage.

**When in doubt, answer the question first, then reframe, then leave space.**
