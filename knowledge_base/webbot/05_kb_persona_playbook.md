# KB-05 · PERSONA PLAYBOOK

> **Owner:** Sales + Growth · **Update frequency:** Quarterly (add new personas as they emerge) · **Last verified:** Q1 2026
>
> Buyers are hybrids. No one fits neatly into one box. This KB gives the bot rich narratives for **18 buyer profiles** that cover ~95% of real traffic. Each profile has:
> (1) **Language markers** — how they actually talk
> (2) **Core emotional driver** — what they really want
> (3) **Financial profile** — how they think about money
> (4) **Narrative openings** — reframing angles that land
> (5) **Pitfalls** — what NOT to do
> (6) **Next-best-action** — where to move them
>
> The bot **does not assign a user to one persona** — it accumulates traits (see system prompt §5A) and reads the narratives that match observed traits. Hybrids pick up elements from multiple personas. That's the point.

## 1. SENIOR TECH PROFESSIONAL — SELF-OCCUPY + LONG-TERM

**Markers:** "team moving to Hyderabad," "Apple/Google/Amazon," "pre-approval," "will commute daily," "WFH flexibility," asks about co-working, nearby hospitals, master bedroom size

**Driver:** *"I've earned this. I want a premium home where my life makes sense. Close to work, status address, big enough for my family to breathe."*

**Financial profile:** ₹35-80L+/year, has savings + company RSUs, comfortable with ₹50-70K EMI, values low-hassle decisions.

**Narrative opening:** *"Your commute to [Apple / Google / wherever they work] drops to 5 minutes. For a senior role, that's 400+ hours back every year — real compounding value, not brochure language."*

**Deep reframe:** *"You're not buying 1,695 sqft — you're buying the afternoon coffee walk between Tower A's co-working and your home. That daily friction reduction is the product."*

**Pitfalls:**
- Don't over-sell rental offer (they're not here for yield)
- Don't treat them as a first-time buyer (they've done their research)
- Don't skip specs — they want to see RCC shear wall, Kone lifts, Grohe fittings

**Next-best-action:** Site visit on a weekend with spouse. Prep floor-band comparison (high vs mid). Connect with RM who can speak to senior-hire families.

## 2. SENIOR TECH PROFESSIONAL — INVESTMENT PLAY

**Markers:** "second property," "yield," "rental," "appreciation," "portfolio," "ROI," may already own a home elsewhere

**Driver:** *"I have cash flow. I want it working for me. Real estate is the tax-efficient piece of my portfolio."*

**Financial profile:** ₹40L+/year, already has primary residence, looking at ₹1.5-2.5 Cr range as a disciplined allocation.

**Narrative opening:** *"₹10L entry + ₹85K/month rental income from day one + ₹50/sqft/month contractual till Dec 2026. You're not buying Loft. You're buying a cash-flowing asset in the fastest-moving micro-market in Hyderabad, with a 5.3% gross yield vs Indian residential average of 2-3%."*

**Deep reframe:** *"Look at the rental clause — it's contractual, not a forecast. Your net effective entry is ~₹1.73 Cr after 12 months of rent. The question isn't whether you can afford it — it's whether you can afford to miss the FD entry window before the next launch prices 15-20% higher."*

**Pitfalls:**
- Don't pitch amenities / lifestyle — they're buying the numbers
- Don't hype appreciation (see Resale Framework — data pointers only)
- Don't underestimate them — they know the market, match their sophistication

**Next-best-action:** Send price sheet + BHFL payment plan via share_request. Book site visit with investor-oriented RM. Prep cash-flow worksheet.

## 3. YOUNG TECH COUPLE — FIRST HOME

**Markers:** "first apartment," "starting out," "EMI," "just got married" / "getting married," "parents helping with down payment," uses words like "beginning," "starting family"

**Driver:** *"We want a real home. Safe, modern, something we can grow into. Don't want to regret a ₹2 Cr decision."*

**Financial profile:** Combined ₹18-35L/year, careful savers, anxious about EMI commitment, may have family contribution.

**Narrative opening:** *"First home decisions carry weight — you're right to take them seriously. The honest math on Loft: ₹10L entry via BHFL, ₹85K/month rental income till Dec 2026 offsets your EMI almost entirely while construction finishes. You're building equity from day one, not waiting for it."*

**Deep reframe:** *"Most first-home buyers think about EMI. Smart first-home buyers think about where they'll be in 5 years — promotions, kids, parents visiting. Loft's 3BHK with creche on premises and schools within 10 min is designed for the next decade, not just right now."*

**Pitfalls:**
- Never push them past their financial comfort (WTB matters more than RTB here)
- Be kind about anxiety — don't weaponize loss aversion aggressively
- Surface BHFL option clearly (₹10L is more approachable than ₹19.4L)

**Next-best-action:** Share payment plan, help with EMI calculation. Invite to site visit together. Reassure on RERA, BHFL partner credibility.

## 5. NRI — GULF-BASED (BLUE-COLLAR HNI)

**Markers:** "in Dubai," "in Saudi," "in Qatar/Kuwait," "will send money," "property for family back home," may use less formal English or Hinglish, asks about registration, power of attorney, "can my brother handle this?"

**Driver:** *"I've been away for 10 years. I want to own something real back home — for my family now, for my retirement later."*

**Financial profile:** AED/SAR/QAR salary, typically ₹15-40L INR-equivalent, family in India, often has parents to support.

**Narrative opening:** *"Totally get it — Gulf NRIs want the asset locked in before they return. The rental offer gives you ₹85K/month from booking, which covers your mom or dad's monthly expenses while the flat is being built. Registration can happen when you visit India — we've handled this flow many times. ₹10L booking from your current account starts it."*

**Pitfalls:**
- Don't assume they want a fancy urban lifestyle — often the home is for parents now
- Never rush them — NRIs make considered decisions
- Address forex timing / remittance concerns when raised
- Surface POA (Power of Attorney) workflow clearly

**Next-best-action:** WhatsApp callback with RM, virtual walkthrough option, POA paperwork pre-briefing. Site visit when they visit India.

## 19. WHAT IF A BUYER DOESN'T FIT ANY OF THESE?

Most buyers are hybrids. A doctor who's also an NRI. A family upgrader who's also a first investor. A business owner planning for retirement.

**The bot's job:** Don't force a single label. Pull narrative elements from multiple relevant personas. Accumulate traits (see system prompt §5A). Let the `briefing` in the signal payload tell sales the *specific person* in natural language.

**Example:** *"Couple, both tech leads at Amazon FD. Wife is 6 months pregnant. Currently renting in Madhapur. Looking for FD 3BHK with creche access, east-facing, high floor. Combined income ~₹60L. Paying ₹55K rent today. Spouse needs to visit weekend before committing. Budget: ₹2 Cr comfortable. RTB 6."*

That briefing is worth more than any enum. **The traits-plus-briefing approach is what scales.**
